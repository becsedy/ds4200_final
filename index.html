Risk Factors Impacting the Prevalence of Diabetes in the United States

Group 26: Benjamin Ecsedy, Andrew Loose, Yash Nalluri

Diabetes and Its Importance

Diabetes refers to a set of diseases that results in high blood glucose, coming in two main types: type 1 and type 2. Millions globally are affected by diabetes, which can result in severe long-term complications such as heart disease, kidney failure, and nerve damage.

Many individuals, especially in the U.S., face significant financial burdens due to diabetes treatment. Understanding the contributing factors—from age and obesity to genetic history and physical inactivity—is essential for preventing and managing this condition.

Our Dataset

We use the "Diabetes 012 Health Indicators" dataset from the CDC’s BRFSS 2015 dataset. It includes 253,680 observations and 22 attributes covering risk behaviors, chronic conditions, and healthcare usage.

For our analysis, we used a stratified 10,000-sample subset while maintaining diagnostic proportions. The dataset contains both categorical and continuous health indicators, making it well-suited for feature importance analysis.

  • 0: No Diabetes
  • 1: Prediabetic
  • 2: Diagnosed Diabetes
